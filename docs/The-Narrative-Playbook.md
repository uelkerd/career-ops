# The Narrative Playbook: Airtight Career & Visa Story (2019–2026)

This playbook contains the exact scripts and psychological framing required to defend your career timeline, remote work history, and visa status against any skeptical German hiring manager. 

## The Golden Rules
1. **Never admit to working 40 hours/week before March 2025.** All remote roles (Sensgreen, DHB Bank) were "highly efficient, asynchronous work structured to comply with the 120-day student limit."
2. **Lean heavily into § 20 Abs. 3 AufenthG.** This specific Job Seeker Visa grants *unrestricted* work rights, which perfectly explains your timeline at MBition and your current consulting work.

## The Timeline & Scripts

### 1. Sensgreen (Late 2019 - 2021)
**The Scenario:** You moved to Germany in late 2020 for your Master's while still employed by an Abu Dhabi company.
**The Script:** *"I have a deep track record of managing asynchronous, remote operations. When I moved to Germany in late 2020 to begin my Master's at Humboldt-Universität, I seamlessly brought my remote role at Sensgreen with me. Because the work was fully asynchronous, it was easily structured to fit within my legal 120-day student working limits."*

### 2. DHB Bank (2021 - 2023)
**The Scenario:** Working for a Dutch bank from Berlin, paid via Turkey.
**The Script:** *"Building on that remote experience, I was contracted by DHB Bank while pursuing my Master's. Since their HQ is in Rotterdam and I was based in Berlin, they structured my employment through their Istanbul representative office. This allowed me to work fully remotely, architecting their Cloud QA systems, while remaining strictly compliant with my German Student Visa working hours."*

### 3. MBition & Graduation (Late 2023 - Dec 2025)
**The Scenario:** You worked at MBition until December 2025, but graduated in March 2025. 
**The Script:** *"When my DHB Bank contract concluded, I was recruited locally in Berlin by MBition. Because I was finalizing my Master's thesis, we initially structured this as a flexible student contract. I graduated in March 2025 and seamlessly transitioned to the standard 18-month post-grad Job Seeker Visa. A massive advantage of this specific visa (§ 20 Abs. 3 AufenthG) is that it grants completely unrestricted work rights in Germany. This allowed me to legally continue my engineering contract with MBition full-time until the project wrapped up in December 2025, deliberately delaying a Blue Card application until I found my next long-term home."*

### 4. The Gap & Visa Urgency (Jan 2026 - Present)
**The Scenario:** Unemployed since December, looking for work, visa expires in August 2026.
**The Script:** *"When the MBition project concluded in December 2025, I used the continued flexibility of my Job Seeker Visa's unrestricted work rights to take on independent consulting projects rather than rushing into the first available job. I work under NDAs, but I've been building AWS CI/CD pipelines and CDD automation workflows for boutique tech firms. My visa is valid until the end of August 2026, so I am entering the final 3-month window. Because I hold a German Master's degree, converting my current visa to a Blue Card is a fast-tracked formality (no ZAV priority check needed), so I am looking to sign a contract quickly to initiate that simple paperwork."*

## The Socratic Stress-Test Framework
When preparing for an interview, the AI Interview Coach will use the **Socratic Method** to aggressively attack your timeline. It will:
1. Probe for legal inconsistencies (e.g., "How did you work full-time on a student visa?").
2. Probe for psychological weaknesses (e.g., "Why didn't MBition hire you permanently?").
3. Probe for technical proof (e.g., "What exactly did you do in your consulting gap?").

**Defending the "Why didn't MBition keep you?" question:**
*"I was brought in specifically to architect the aiFeeder backend and establish the DOORS Next/ASPICE integrations. By December, that core infrastructure was fully deployed and operational. At that point, the role shifted from 'building' to 'maintenance'. I realized my passion lies in building and scaling infrastructure, so I made the strategic choice to step away, do some independent consulting, and look for a senior Platform Engineering role where I can take true ownership."*
