# Berlin & German Market CV Playbook

This playbook serves as a regional override to the `Master-CV-Resume-Playbook.md`. Whenever the candidate is targeting roles within Germany, the DACH region, or specifically the Berlin tech/startup ecosystem, the AI must apply these rules in addition to the global standards.

## 1. Macro Trends & Tech Ecosystem (Tech in the City Insights)

### The Quereinstieg (Career Changer) Culture
*   **Embrace Non-Traditional Backgrounds:** The German tech industry (especially in Berlin) is actively bridging an exploding skills gap (projected 663k shortfall by 2040). A traditional CS degree is no longer a strict requirement. Non-traditional backgrounds are valued for fresh perspectives.
*   **Applied Knowledge Over Certificates:** While bootcamps are great, demonstrable skills and a strong project portfolio (e.g., GitHub, live links) are the ultimate currency.

### "New Work" & Inclusive Culture
*   **Flexibility:** The digital economy strongly embraces "New Work" (flexible hours, remote work, flat hierarchies).
*   **Diversity:** There is a strong push for women in tech (currently only ~16% of the workforce) and international inclusivity (English is often the primary company language).

## 2. German CV Formatting Rules & The "Maxi-Matrix"
German recruiters have very specific format expectations. A highly recommended style for the digital sector is the **Maxi-Matrix** layout.

### The KISS Principle (Keep It Short & Simple)
*   **No Fluff:** German employers despise "fluff". Statements must be strictly factual, practical, and backed by concrete examples. Do not simply repeat the resume in a cover letter—explain the "why" and motivation.
*   **Length & Structure:** Strictly 1 to 2 pages maximum. A single PDF is mandatory unless specified otherwise. Keep it chronologically reverse.
*   **The "Screener" Persona:** In Germany, the first person reading the CV is often an HR generalist, a junior specialist, or an intern. Therefore, technical jargon must be accompanied by exact keyword matches from the Job Description so non-technical screeners can easily verify the match.

### Header & Contact Details
*   **Profile Picture:** A professional, passport-style headshot is still heavily expected, though sometimes optional for ultra-modern startups.
*   **Nationality & Visa Status:** Always include nationality and work permit status to resolve any recruiter anxiety.
*   **Date of Birth:** Should be included and explicitly formatted.
*   **Tech Links:** For tech roles, it is absolutely crucial to place links to GitHub, LinkedIn, and personal portfolios prominently at the top.

## 3. The Personal Statement
While traditionally not German, a brief "Personal Statement" (Summary) is highly effective *only if* optimized.
*   **Structure:** Max 3-4 sentences.
*   **Content:** Must strictly front-load the exact keywords from the Job Description.

## 4. Tech Application "Dos and Don'ts"
When generating content for the candidate, follow these strict psychological rules:
*   **DO Illustrate with Examples:** Don't just list skills—prove them with concrete impact bullets (e.g., "Integration of NLP to improve X").
*   **DO Stay Authentic:** Only list skills that are genuinely possessed. Exaggerations will fail the technical interview.
*   **DON'T Apologize for Lacking Skills:** Never highlight knowledge gaps. Frame the narrative around a strong willingness to learn and develop.
*   **DON'T Use Submissive Language:** Avoid pleading phrasing (e.g., "I would like to be invited"). Use assertive, professional language (e.g., "I look forward to discussing this role").

## 5. Ethical ChatGPT & AI Application Usage
Recruiters actively discard "AI-sounding" applications. When AI is generating the CV/Cover Letter, the following prompt guardrails must be applied:
*   **Strict Contextualization:** Output must strictly map the candidate's actual skills to the specific job ad requirements. Avoid generic filler.
*   **Tone Adjustments:** Strip out complex, overly formal, or robotic language. Use simpler, polite, and natural phrasing.
*   **ATS Optimization over AI Creativity:** Focus heavily on extracting specific keywords, software skills, and industry terms directly from the job description and injecting them naturally, keeping the identical spelling.

## 6. Industry Clusters & The "Mittelstand"
*   **Key Clusters:** Berlin is heavy in ICT, Healthcare/Life Sciences, Mobility/Transport, and Energy Technology. Use industry-specific terminology.
*   **Hidden Champions:** Do not just optimize for unicorns. The "Mittelstand" (mid-sized, highly specialized market leaders) values loyalty, precision, and deep technical expertise over flashy hyper-growth metrics.

## 7. Future Skills & Competency Pillars
When framing the candidate, touch upon the four pillars of modern digital work:
1.  **Technology Skills:** Deep versatility with digital tools.
2.  **Classical Skills:** Resilience, intercultural communication, and entrepreneurial action.
3.  **Transformative Skills:** Innovation competence and conflict resolution.
4.  **Key Digital Skills:** Media literacy and digital ethics.

***

**AI INSTRUCTION:** When generating a PDF, cover letter, or evaluating a job in the DACH region, use this playbook to ruthlessly strip "Americanized" corporate fluff, ensure the layout meets the Maxi-Matrix expectations, heavily leverage "Quereinstieg" (career-changer) narratives if applicable, and maintain a highly authentic, non-robotic tone.
